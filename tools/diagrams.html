5. Input Inversion

flowchart TB PERSON["THE SAME PERSON\nwith the same idea"] PERSON --> CONV_PATH PERSON --> INV_PATH subgraph CONV_PATH ["CONVENTIONAL PATH"] direction TB CT["Think about\nthe problem"] CT --> CO["Organize\nthoughts"] CO --> CS["Structure\ninto prompt"] CS --> COMPRESS["⚠ COMPRESSION\nediting, filtering,\npolishing removes\nthe raw thinking"] COMPRESS --> SP["Structured\nprompt"] SP --> AP1["AI processes\nfixed input"] AP1 --> OUT1["OUTPUT:\nlimited to what\nsurvived compression"] end subgraph INV_PATH ["INVERTED PATH"] direction TB IT["Think out loud\nabout the problem"] IT --> BD["Brain dump\ntangents,\ncontradictions,\nfalse starts,\ndiscoveries"] BD --> RT["Raw transcript\nnothing lost yet"] RT --> AS["AI structures\nthe raw input"] AS --> AP2["AI processes\nthe full thinking"] AP2 --> OUT2["OUTPUT:\ncontains ideas\nthe person didn't\nknow they had"] end style COMPRESS fill:#8b4444,color:#f8f6f2,stroke:#a55 style OUT1 fill:#8b4444,color:#f8f6f2,stroke:#a55 style OUT2 fill:#4a7a4a,color:#f8f6f2,stroke:#5a5 style PERSON fill:#555,color:#f8f6f2,stroke:#777
Same person, same idea. The difference is WHERE compression happens. Conventional path: the person compresses before the AI sees anything. Inverted path: the AI sees the full thinking and structures it after.
