AI Accommodation: The Foundations

Self-paced · 3 weeks · $299 · Designed for designers

This is not ten hours of video. It is three weeks of structured practice using a gradual release model: demonstration, guided practice, independent practice. Every lesson is built around work you do in your own tools, on your own projects. You finish with a working system, not a playlist you watched.

Three Weeks. Three Domains.

architectureWeek 1: Task Decomposition

Demo, guided practice, independent practice

Your prompts keep getting longer and your output keeps getting worse. The problem is structural: compound instructions degrade in AI the same way they degrade in a classroom. You learn to decompose, run independently, and synthesize. The AI gives you depth. You provide the coherence.

Deliverable: A decomposed workflow for one real project, with before/after comparison.

grainWeek 2: Input Inversion

Demo, guided practice, independent practice

Structured prompts compress what makes your thinking yours. Unstructured input (talking, brainstorming, arguing with yourself) contains richer raw material. You learn to dump your thinking and let the tool find the structure. The ideas that emerge are yours because the raw material was yours.

Deliverable: An input practice for one real project, with a recorded brain dump and its structured output.

fingerprintWeek 3: Voice Governance

Demo, guided practice, independent practice

All AI copy sounds the same because voice constraints are applied after generation, not during. You learn to extract how you actually talk (from conversation, not published writing), codify it as generation rules, and apply those rules before the first word is produced. The output sounds like you because the constraints occupied the space where the AI's defaults would have gone.

Deliverable: A voice protocol extracted from your own speech patterns, tested against real output.

From Week 1

What the difference looks like.

Students run the same brand brief two ways. One compound prompt. Five decomposed prompts. The difference is not subtle.

compressCompound Prompt

Logo Direction

"Hand-Lettered Wordmark. 'VOSS TYPE' as a single lettered unit. Not a font. Drawn by hand, then digitized with minimal cleanup. Keep the stroke variation, the ink traps, the slight baseline wobble."

open_in_fullDecomposed Prompt

Logo Direction

"The mark should feel like pressure. The kind of weight you get from a wide-nib tool pressed hard into paper. Draw it twenty times. The consistency across all twenty is the mark. Keep the consistency, release the variations. If it looks like a portfolio sample, it has failed. If it looks like a signature, it is working."

Same brief. Same model. The compound version described a logo. The decomposed version described what the logo needs to feel like and why. One is a deliverable. The other is a design philosophy you can act on.
