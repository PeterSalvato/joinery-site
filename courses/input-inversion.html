Input Inversion

Self-paced · 3 weeks · $299 · Pedagogy: Experiential / Contrastive Learning
In Development

The situation you recognize

You have the idea but can't execute. The instinct is to organize first: outline, structure, plan. By the time you've formatted the thought, you've lost the thing you were trying to capture.

This happens to writers, designers, strategists. You sit down to work and spend the first hour trying to arrange what you already know into something presentable. The raw version was richer. The structured version is thinner. And you can feel the difference, even if you can't name it.

What this means for AI work

Structured prompts compress what makes your thinking yours. When you organize your ideas into bullet points before giving them to AI, you strip out the texture, the hesitation, the unexpected connections. The AI then works with your outline instead of your thinking.

Input Inversion reverses the direction. You dump raw: talk, ramble, contradict yourself, think out loud. The AI finds the structure inside what you said. The ideas that come back are yours because the raw material was yours. The AI did organization, not ideation.

What you'll build

A working input practice for your own creative work. You'll record a raw brain dump on a real project, run it through the inversion process, and compare the structured output against what you would have written from a blank page. The deliverable is a repeatable pipeline: dump, extract, structure. You use it the day after the course ends.

Deliverable: An input inversion pipeline tested on your own project, with a recorded brain dump and its structured output as evidence.

Why this teaching method

This course uses experiential and contrastive learning. You do the wrong thing first, on purpose. You write a structured prompt, then dump the same idea raw, then compare the two outputs side by side. The difference is not explained to you. You see it in your own work. The contrastive method works here because the gap between structured-prompt output and inverted-input output is immediately visible once you hold them next to each other. Telling someone about the gap is less effective than making them produce both versions.
