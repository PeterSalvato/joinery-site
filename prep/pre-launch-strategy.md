## 1. LinkedIn: From Zero to Authority (The "Attunement" Strategy)
*You cannot shout into a void. You must enter existing conversations.*

- **The Hook (Problem-First):** Do not post about "Accommodation Design." Post about **"Prompt Bloat."** 
    - *Example:* "I spent 25 years as a CD. I'm watching brilliant designers lose their voice to 1,000-word prompts that produce 100-word slop. The problem isn't the model. It's the structure."
- **The 5-3-1 Daily Routine (30 mins):**
    - **5 Comments:** Find 5 posts by design leaders (Victore, Draplin, Chris Do, etc.) or people complaining about AI. Leave a high-signal comment that uses your "Special Ed" framing (e.g., "This looks like a processing delay issue. In the classroom, we'd decompose this. Why aren't we doing that with LLMs?").
    - **3 Replies:** Engage with anyone who replies to you.
    - **1 Post:** Share one "Lesson from the Bench." Use a real image (a screenshot of a "Hot Sauce" prompt vs. output).
- **The "DOI Flex":** Once a week, mention that this isn't "prompt engineering"—it's grounded in 6 peer-reviewed whitepapers. This separates you from the "AI Guru" noise immediately.

## 2. The Victore Contingency
*He is your "Master" in the lineage. Use that connection regardless of his reply.*

### If he replies (The Partnership):
- **The Ask:** "James, I'm building the school I wish I had at SVA. I've codified the CD-level synthesis into a methodology. Can I run a 30-minute 'Roughing It' workshop for your audience? No pitch, just pure structural value."
- **The Goal:** Borrow his trust to seed your first 50-100 subscribers.

### If he doesn't reply (The Lineage):
- **The Pivot:** "I studied under James Victore at SVA. He taught me that design is about the 'who,' not the 'what.' When I moved into AI, I realized the tools were killing the 'who.' So I built Joinery to save it."
- **Action:** Tag him in a post about what you learned from him and how it applies to AI. It's a tribute, not a "pick me." His audience will see it.

## 3. The Lead Magnet: "The Wrong Question"
*Your essay is currently a destination. It needs to be a gate.*
