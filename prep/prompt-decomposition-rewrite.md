# Site Copy Rewrite: Prompt Decomposition as Creative Direction

**Status:** STAGED / PREP
**Objective:** Elevate "Task Decomposition" from a clerical prompting technique to a directorial methodology, using formal special education terminology.

---

## 1. Homepage Curriculum Section (The "What It Is")

**Current:** 
> "The founding reframe. Ask what the system needs, not how to constrain it. Task decomposition as accommodation."

**Proposed Rewrite:**
> "The founding reframe. Grounded in special education pedagogy, we don't ask how to constrain the AI; we ask what its processing reality requires. Task decomposition isn't about writing shorter prompts—it's about isolating dimensions so you can apply human judgment where the model applies defaults."

---

## 2. Foundations Sales Page: Week 1 Description

**Current:** 
> "Week 1: Task Decomposition — Your prompts keep getting longer and your output keeps getting worse. The problem is structural. You learn to decompose, run independently, and synthesize."

**Proposed Rewrite:**
> "Week 1: Task Decomposition — Your prompts keep getting longer while your output keeps getting worse. The problem is structural. When you give an AI five objectives at once, its attention splits, producing competent but shallow 'slop.' 
>
> You will learn to decompose compound tasks into discrete, single-objective specialists. The AI provides the deep raw material. You provide the synthesis. You aren't just prompting; you are learning to direct."

---

## 3. The "Hot Sauce" Demo Intro (The Proof)

**Context:** This introduces the visual comparison of the 3 outputs on the sales page.

**Proposed Rewrite:**
> "This is the methodology in practice. 
> 
> On the left: The AI trying to process a compound brief all at once. The output is generic because the system's attention is overloaded.
> 
> In the middle: The same brief, decomposed into five independent specialists. The depth increases, but the AI's attempt to synthesize them lacks point-of-view.
> 
> On the right: The human Creative Director stepping in to synthesize the specialist reports, overriding consensus, and making the structural decisions the AI cannot make."
