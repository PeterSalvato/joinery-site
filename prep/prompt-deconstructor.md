# The Joinery Prompt Deconstructor
**Subtitle:** A Master Creative’s guide to escaping the "Compound Prompt" trap.

## The Problem: The Consensus Trap
When you give an AI five objectives at once (e.g., "Write a brand brief that is gritty but high-end, references 1950s woodblocks, and sounds stoic"), the model’s attention splits. To avoid failing, it seeks the **Consensus**—the average of all its training data. This is how you get AI "Slop."

## The Joinery Move: Isolation for Judgment
We use the rigorous mechanics of **special education pedagogy** to accommodate the model’s processing profile. We don't write prompts; we build **Scaffolds.**

### Step 1: Decompose (Isolate the Dimensions)
Stop trying to solve the "Whole." Isolate the dimensions into independent Specialists:
- **Specialist A (Strategy):** Competitive landscape and "Craft" positioning.
- **Specialist B (Visuals):** Woodblock textures, limited palettes, and printer's marks.
- **Specialist C (Voice):** Stoic register and "Earned" vocabulary.

### Step 2: Extract the Raw Material
Run each specialist independently. They will go 10x deeper than a compound prompt because their "Attention Budget" is focused on exactly one objective.

### Step 3: The Directorial Synthesis (The Scaffold)
This is the **Joinery Secret.** You don't paste the results together. You use a **Coordination Layer (The Interviewer)**—an assistant that reads the specialist reports and asks *you* the three hardest questions:
- *"Specialist A says we are high-end, but Specialist B suggests dirt-under-the-fingernails visuals. Peter, which tension are we leaning into?"*

### The Result:
You provide the **Judgment.** The system provides the **Structure.** You just did 20 hours of CD-level strategy in 20 minutes, and the identity remains 100% yours.
