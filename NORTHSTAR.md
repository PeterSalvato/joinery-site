## The Mission

Joinery builds structure for preserving and extending creative thought through AI.

Not faster output. Not better prompts. A different relationship with the tool — one where the quality and specificity of your thinking survives contact with AI and comes back extended, not averaged.

---

## The Audience

Joinery is for anyone whose primary output is original thought.

Specifically: brand designers, authors, illustrators, copywriters, brand narrative writers, creative directors, musicians, architects, educators, strategists. Anyone whose work lives or dies on the quality of their original thinking, and who has felt that thinking get flattened by AI.

**The site speaks in practitioner-specific language.** These people do not identify as "creatives" or "people who think creatively." They are authors. They are brand designers. They are illustrators. The copy names them specifically so each one sees themselves.

The Designer Track is the first track — not the only one, not the defining one. A novelist and an illustrator should feel as welcome as a brand designer.

## The Mechanism

Joinery is built on mutual attunement — the same principle that governs special education pedagogy.

In a classroom: the teacher reads how the student actually processes and designs structure around that reality. The student, in return, shows what they actually know when the structure meets them where they are. Both sides are accommodated. The relationship is generative.

With AI: you read how the AI actually processes and design your input around that reality (Accommodation Design). In return, when you give AI your full explanation — unfiltered, uncompressed, no shorthand — it can work with your actual thinking rather than defaulting to averages. That full explanation is the source code. It's richer than a brief, richer than notes, richer than a structured prompt, because you had to say everything.

This is the cognitive partnership. Division of labor:
- **Human:** wild ideation, judgment, aesthetic sense, synthesis
- **AI:** pattern recognition, holding context, finding structure in raw material
- **The structure (protocols, syntax, tools):** the container that makes the partnership consistent and cumulative

Neither side is doing the other's work. Both sides are doing what they do best. Together you reach further than either alone.

## The Architecture

**Prosthetic Cognition is the destination.** The other five domains are how you get there.

The six domains are not equal peers. They form a hierarchy:

- **Accommodation Design** — ask what AI needs from you to produce what you meant
- **Input Inversion** — dump your raw thinking; let AI find the structure in it
- **Voice Governance** — build your voice into the constraints before AI starts generating
- **Semantic Hierarchy** — mark what actually matters; AI treats everything equally
- **Lens Extraction** — turn your taste into criteria you can actually test
- **Prosthetic Cognition** — the result of all five: a cognitive partnership where your thinking is preserved, structured, and extended through AI

The school teaches you to build the first five. The sixth is what you've built when they work together.

## The Emotional Arc

**The hook is survival.** People come because they're losing something — their voice, their specificity, the thing that makes their work theirs. "Your work should survive your tools" gets them in the door.

**The payoff is extension.** What they discover is that the methodology isn't just defensive. When both sides are attuned, you reach further than you could alone. The problem isn't just solved — the ceiling lifts.

Copy should honor this arc. The problem section earns the survival hook. The methodology section begins the shift toward extension. The course descriptions name what you gain, not just what you stop losing.

---

## The Category

Joinery is not competing with AI courses. It is not a prompting guide. It is not a tool.

The closest existing category is creative infrastructure — the systems, tools, and practices that serious creative practitioners build to do their best work. Zettelkasten for writers. Scrivener for novelists. Voice protocols for editors. Joinery is that kind of infrastructure, built specifically for the AI layer of creative practice.

No one else is building this. The AI labs are building for reliable output. Reliable means averaged. For creative work, averaged is the wrong goal. Joinery is the first research-backed methodology built specifically for creative practitioners who need the opposite.

## What the Site Does NOT Say

- The site does not say "designers" as an audience label. It says "creatives" or names specific practitioners.
- The site does not use methodology vocabulary as the hook: no "attunement," "accommodation design," "input inversion," "prosthetic cognition" as entry-level terms. These appear as course names and research anchors, not as the way in.
- The site does not frame AI as magic to hand work off to. The methodology is about staying in the work, not leaving it.
- The site does not over-promise. No "10x your output." No "like magic." No hype language.
- The site does not lecture. Every page gives the reader something useful — a question to ask, a move to try, a way to see their situation differently.
