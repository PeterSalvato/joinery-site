Courses

Built by a trained educator whose methodology comes from special education pedagogy and federal disability law. Every course is grounded in published cognitive science research (six whitepapers with DOIs, linked on the research page) and uses the gradual release model: demonstration, guided practice, independent practice. The question underneath every course is the one that governs IEPs in the classroom: what does this system actually need to succeed at this task? You apply every course to your own real work, not fictional examples.

Designer Track

A seven-course ladder for working designers absorbing AI into their practice. Start with Fundamentals, which introduces the core methodology across three domains at entry level. Each subsequent course takes one domain and goes deep: the standing practice, the infrastructure, the advanced applications. Foundations gives you the first move in each domain. The practitioner courses give you the full practice.

01 · AI Accommodation: The Foundations

Building · Self-paced · 3 weeks · $299 · Designers

Designers who use AI lose authorship. This course teaches you to use the attunement skill you already have to stay in the author's seat. Conversation architecture, ideation compilation, design gates, first AI-assisted artifact shipped on your real project.

Research anchor: Accommodation Design · Citations include Sweller, Cowan, Murdock, Liu et al.

02 · Voice Governance

Self-paced · 3 weeks (planned) · Designers

AI writing sounds like AI because it learned from published performance, not real voice. This course teaches you to capture your actual voice from conversations, build protocols that govern AI output during generation, and verify the result before it ships.

Research anchor: Voice Governance · Citations include Bock, Pennebaker, Flower & Hayes
In Development

03 · Input Inversion

Self-paced · 3 weeks (planned) · Designers

Structured prompts cap your output at the level of the prompt. Unstructured human thinking produces richer AI output when you invert the input pipeline. This course teaches you to use your raw thinking as the corpus and let structure emerge.

Research anchor: Input Inversion · Citations include Bereiter & Scardamalia, Flower & Hayes
In Development

04 · Lens Extraction

Self-paced · 3 weeks (planned) · Designers

"Is this good?" is the wrong question. Decompose evaluation into independent lenses drawn from real practitioners. This course teaches you to build evaluation systems that catch what single-prompt review misses.

Research anchor: Lens Extraction · Citations include Wong et al. (task analysis as evidence-based practice)
In Development

05 · Semantic Hierarchy

Self-paced · 3 weeks (planned) · Designers

AI memory loses what mattered. This course teaches you to structure what the AI should remember, so your working context survives across sessions instead of starting from zero every time.

Research anchor: Semantic Flattening · Citations on context degradation and human-marked importance
In Development

06 · Prosthetic Cognition

Self-paced · 3 weeks (planned) · Designers

AI is not a replacement for thinking or a tool beside thinking. It is a cognitive extension: the climbing harness, not the climber, not the mountain. This course teaches you to hold the distinction structurally and keep judgment in the human.

Research anchor: Prosthetic Cognition · Citations from extended cognition and cognitive offloading research
In Development

07 · Coordinator Building

Self-paced · 4 weeks (planned) · Designers · Capstone

Stop manually repeating patterns. Build infrastructure that embodies your workflow. This capstone course teaches you to materialize your practice as skills, coordinators, and protocols that serve you across every project.

Research anchor: synthesizes all six Joinery whitepapers into applied infrastructure practice
