AI flattens. Your work should survive it.

The methodology for creatives who refuse to let the AI decide what they meant.

You're a brand designer with a brief, an author with a chapter, an illustrator with a direction, a copywriter with a campaign, a creative director with a team. You try AI. The output has the right shape but it isn't quite you. Maybe it happened gradually: each session a little further from what you meant, nothing you could point to. Or it was immediate. The first draft came back and felt like someone else wrote it. Either way, the AI filled the gaps with its defaults. Its defaults aren't yours. They're what millions of similar projects look like when averaged together.

More prompting doesn't fix this. The problem starts before the prompt.

The AI labs are building systems for reliable output. Reliable means averaged. For code, that works. For creative work, averaged is the death of the idea. Joinery doesn't ask how to make the AI reliable. It asks how to keep your intent alive.

Joinery was founded by Peter Salvato, a design engineer who spent twenty-five years reading rooms: construction sites, classrooms, enterprise platforms, kitchens, printshops, recording studios, a novel. In a special education classroom in Brooklyn, he learned that designing around how the student actually processes is federal law. Three years ago he recognized the same constraints in AI systems and started building around them. Six published papers with DOIs document the methodology. The courses teach it.

You already have this skill. DJs call it reading the room. Teachers call it reading the classroom. Designers call it reading the client. Writers call it reading their reader. Whatever you call it: figuring out what something needs before you start. Joinery teaches you to apply it to AI.

01 Accommodation Design Ask what AI needs from you to produce what you meant. Build to that answer.
02 Input Inversion Dump your raw thinking. Let the AI find the structure in it.
03 Lens Extraction Bring the perspectives you trust into the process. Run your work through each one before it leaves your hands.
04 Voice Governance Build your voice into the setup before the first word. What comes out sounds like you — the AI's defaults had nowhere to go.
05 Semantic Hierarchy AI treats everything as equally important. You mark what actually matters.
06 Coordinator Building Wire the practices into one pipeline — or build one your whole team runs from. The scaffolding handles itself; your attention stays on the work.
