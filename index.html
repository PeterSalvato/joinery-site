A school for creative practitioners who use AI.

Other courses teach you the tools. Joinery teaches the thinking underneath: a structural methodology for AI-assisted creative work that doesn't expire when the tools change next quarter.

Creatives face two fears at once. Don't use AI, get replaced. Use it, and everything you make looks like everything everyone else is making.

The tool tutorials won't fix this. The prompt engineering courses won't fix this. The problem is structural, and it requires a methodology, not a hack.

Accommodation Design

Designers already have the skill this methodology depends on. You attune to clients, audiences, customers every day. You read what they need and design around that reading. Joinery teaches you to apply that same attunement skill to AI as a new receiving system.

The AI governance field asks one question: how do we control what the model does? Accommodation Design asks a different one: what does the system actually need to do this job well?

That question comes from special education classrooms, where designing around the processing reality of the receiving system is federal law. The methodology is grounded in published cognitive science research (six whitepapers with DOIs) and built by a trained educator. Applied to AI, it changes how you structure input, preserve your voice, evaluate output, and keep your judgment in the room.

Six domains. One methodology.

Each domain answers the founding question from a different angle. They build on each other in sequence.

  1. 01

    Accommodation Design

    The founding reframe. Ask what the system needs, not how to constrain it. Task decomposition as accommodation.

  2. 02

    Input Inversion

    Unstructured human input plus structured tools beats structured prompts. The burden of organization belongs to the tooling.

  3. 03

    Prosthetic Cognition

    AI as cognitive extension, not tool and not replacement. A harness that extends reach without climbing for you.

  4. 04

    Semantic Hierarchy

    AI memory flattens everything. Human-marked importance preserves what mattered. Without it, better recall just produces more flattened material.

  5. 05

    Voice Governance

    Constraints applied during generation produce structurally different output than constraints applied after. Voice is structural, not stylistic.

  6. 06

    Lens Extraction

    "Is this good?" is twelve questions. Extract what strong practitioners consistently ask, codify as testable criteria, run independently.

Domains build in sequence. Accommodation Design is the foundation. Lens Extraction is the capstone. Each domain has its own courses across practitioner, director, and educator levels.
