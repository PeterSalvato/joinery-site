The tools get faster. The work gets flatter. The person disappears.

Joinery teaches one methodology for keeping your work yours.

A designer runs AI on a brief and the output covers everything but goes deep on nothing. A Shopify merchant launches a store and the template makes handcrafted product look mass-market. A writer uses AI to draft and the prose comes back competent, smooth, and completely absent of their voice. Three different practitioners, three different tools, the same structural problem: the tool filled the gaps with its own defaults.

More instructions, more templates, more editing after the fact. None of it fixes the problem because the problem starts before production begins. The input was shaped wrong for the system receiving it.

Joinery teaches you to read what the system actually needs and design your input to match. The methodology is called Accommodation Design. It comes from special education pedagogy, where designing around how the student actually processes is federal law. It applies to AI. It applies to eCommerce platforms. It applies to the blank page.

What every offering teaches

Three structural moves, applied to your domain:

Break compound work into parts A compound task splits attention across everything at once. Decompose it into single-objective steps and each one gets real depth. The synthesis is where your judgment becomes the product.
Keep raw thinking in the work Structured input compresses what makes your thinking yours. Start with the raw version. The false starts, the contradictions, the rough edges. Let the structure come from the material.
Build rules that hold your voice Your voice lives in how you actually talk, not in style guides written after the fact. Extract the patterns from real conversation. Load them before production begins. The output sounds like you because the defaults had nowhere to go.
